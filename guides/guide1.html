Windows Optimization

Is your Windows PC feeling sluggish? Here are the essential steps to debloat, optimize, and speed up your system for maximum performance.

1. Debloat Windows

Windows comes with a lot of pre-installed apps and telemetry that you don't need. The most effective way to remove them is using a PowerShell script.

Open PowerShell as Administrator and run the following command to launch the Chris Titus Tech WinUtil (a popular, safe community tool):

irm christitus.com/win | iex

From the utility, you can select "Tweaks" and choose "Desktop" or "Laptop" to automatically apply recommended optimizations.

2. Disable Telemetry & Background Apps

Stop Windows from sending data to Microsoft and running unnecessary apps in the background.

# Disable Telemetry reg add "HKLM\SOFTWARE\Policies\Microsoft\Windows\DataCollection" /v AllowTelemetry /t REG_DWORD /d 0 /f # Disable Background Apps (Global) reg add "HKCU\Software\Microsoft\Windows\CurrentVersion\BackgroundAccessApplications" /v GlobalUserDisabled /t REG_DWORD /d 1 /f

3. Optimize Power Plan

Ensure your CPU is running at its full potential by enabling the "High Performance" or "Ultimate Performance" power plan.

# Enable Ultimate Performance Mode powercfg -duplicatescheme e9a42b02-d5df-448d-aa00-03f14749eb61

After running this, go to Control Panel > Power Options and select "Ultimate Performance".

4. Clean Temporary Files

Free up space and reduce clutter by clearing temp files.

# Clean Temp Folder Remove-Item -Path $env:TEMP\* -Recurse -Force -ErrorAction SilentlyContinue

Regular maintenance is key. Restart your PC once a week and keep your drivers updated!
